Explore my methods

RCA

(Root Cause Analysis)

RCA is a problem-solving sequence that uses deductive logic to uncover causal factors within a problem statement.

This powerful tool helps to uncover the real problem, uncovering the basic need behind a client's stated problem.

Using RCA, saves time, money and design focus while establishing clear communication between client and designer.

The 5 Whys

This method is as simple as it is useful in generating in-depth leads related to the root cause analysis of a problem.

Handled correctly, the five why's is an efficient, useful and simple tool, whose benefits far outweight the costs of execution.

Stakeholder Interviews

A stakeholder is someone with direct interest, input or influence over a project, usually part of the business itself.

Stakeholder interviews reveal the business's perspective on crucial design details - Business goals, project constraints, definitions of success, of failure, existing user data, the attitude and roles of key players within the project, etc.

Explore my research abilities

Qualitative research

I employ this whenever the design process requires development of concepts which are executed by the user in a natural environment.

Qualitative research explores the user's meanings, experiences and views within specific goal-based scenarios.

Interviews

Interviews offer a comfortable setting for the user where invaluable data is recorder in various ways with an explorational purpose.

Focus Groups

It makes use of a controlled group discussion on a specific topic related to the aim of the research.
Multiple types of recording are included to enrich data and allow analysis of participants' body language, tonality and facial expressions.

Card Sorting

A trade mark of UX research. It explores and describes the views, ideas and perspectives of several participants, which are later analyzed for inspiration, insight and feature generation.

Ethnographical research

Research based on observation, with or without designer participation.

Observations aim to record, perceive and, later, analyse user behavior within a natural setting for the user.

ORM
(Online research methods)

Checking whether there is existing, high quality research data, executing traditional and the newest research techniques via the web can save time, effort and energy.
I employ the following:Focus Groups, Interviews, Questionnaires, Surveys, Web-based experiments.

Contextual inquiry

A hollistic approach that follows the day to day activities of the user.
This uncovers the details of work or play within the user's process flows, interactions, protocols and criterias.

Grounded Theory

It aims to explain or theorize the reasons behind a sequence of events or a specific result.
Keywords: Interviews, existing data, identifying patterns.

Phenomenological Research

Uncovers the reason, characteristics and details surrounding a certain phenomenon within the physical or digital world.
A great tool for discovering affordances.

Previous: "Questionnaires"

Quantitative Research

Aims to describe behavior of larger groups of users through numeric and statistical approaches.

The accumulated data is independent from the researcher, so it's displays objective characteristics of reality.

The results can be presented in a visually clear way with little room for arguments as to their validity.

Survey

Is easy to plan, execute and are subject to all advantages good quantitative data has to offer. They inform and guide the design process, validate choices and show insights.

Correlational Research

All about comparing two variables within a system via a statistical relationship.

Results: Identifies and describes the nature of the relationships between parts of the user experience.

Experimental Research

Guided by a hypothesis, this type of research is excellent for early design vision validation, as a hypothesis is either proven or disproven.

Results: Explanations to different phenomena.
Emprirical data that is hard to argue with.
Data that clears, focuses and guides the research and design process forward.

Causal-Comparative Research

Looks to uncover a cause and effect relationship. Via comparing and observing the behavior of two variables under the same effect.

Results: Possible inspiration source for all stages of the design process, possible validation for design decision or feature.

Explore my analytical thinking

QDA
(Qualitative Data Analysis)

This is data that is not easily reduced to numbers. Instead, it rather offers insights into the concepts, opinions, values and behaviors of people in social contexts.

The end product is the explanation or interpretation of the people or situations that've been investigated.

Deductive approach

Using intuition and analytical thinking, I'm able to spot various patterns and correlations in data chunks.

Analytic Induction

Is a great way to discover the minimum necessary conditions needed for an event or phenomenon to happen.

Content analysis

Describes what is the data and what was meant by it.

Narrative Analysis

Every qualitative interview/observation has a narrative attached to it, both for the designer and user.

Core activity: Reformulate the stories presented during the research to get a clearer view and better overal understanding of the intricacies surrounding the activities.

Metaphorical Analysis

Is a simple, yet effective technique which describes and explains an activity, phenomenon or research data using the most appropriate metaphors.

Framework Analysis

Consists of several stages whose goals is to chunk the data into usable variables that can be used to identify keyword and thematic patterns across the data pool.

Quantitative Analysis

Numerical analysis desscribes and explains the research object.

The insights describe this phenomena independently with an objective validation to them.

The ability to expand quantitative data into predictions is a major advantage that I possess.

Distribution Analysis

Is a univariate analysis that explores and describes the frequency distribution of something within a phenomena.

I use it to test pontential solutions, identify winning and losing elements within the design solution.

Central Tendency Analysis

Present the data in the form of an average.

Correlation Analysis

Is a bivariate type analysis, whose main goal is to work out predictions of behavior for two variables based on statistics.

Regression

Regression utilizies statistics to test the relationship between one independent and one dependent variable.

The formula to the right should explain everything.

ANOVA
(Analysis of Variance)

Is a method used to describe the relationship between two or more variables. The end result is a general conclusion that applies to a larger group of people, compared to all methods so far.
This provides a wider view for the direction of the design process and solution.

Word Cloud Analysis

Through specific software, a body of interest is compiled into a word cloud where the most and least frequent words are displayed.
This provides insights, related to the thematic and semantic structure of something.

Heuristic Analysis

It determines whether a product or service fulfills predetermined heuristics (qualitative guidelines).

Heuristics answer whether a product or service is easy to use, efficient, learnable, memorable, safe and accessible.

Explore my testing abilities

Testing

Testing shows the status of the design solution at the moment. It can evaluate how usable, useful, engaging and meaningful a product is to a user.
It shows whether the design is truly goal-oriented or whether it covers the business goals of the client.

It helps answer questions like:

"Why is this not working?"
"How can we make this more user goal oriented?"
"Where is the issue coming from?", etc.

A/B Tests

a.k.a. "Split Testing" is a visual comparison between two possible design paths.

It helps with focusing the design efforts towards the "winner" that will ensure best UX.

URUT (Unmoderated Remote Usability Testing)

This tool allows for overcoming the downsides of moderated usability testing.

It's a' faster, cheaper and better way to test usability with users, using various online tools.

Focus Groups

It makes use of a controlled group discussion on a specific topic related to the aim of the research. It gives. Multiple types of recording are included to enrich data and allow analysis of participants's body language, tonality and facial expressions.

Tree Test

Technique to test out whether the navigation within the design is actually efficient and user goal/task oriented.

Performance Test

The goal is to evaluate the performance of a system with the currently implemented features with an end goal to ensure efficiency for user task completion.

This is the stepping stone for inducing flow and creating a stage for the narrative of the service/product.

5-Second Test

Based on the intricate ways human vision works, this test describes the visual impression that is left by the user after spending 5 seconds viewing whatever.

Brand Perception Test

Strives the attitude of the user towards a brand through words, signs and emotions.

This is done through CSAT's (Customer Satisfaction Survey's)
NPS's (Net Promoter Scores)
Social media and analytic analysis.
