SharePoint - poor man's Webflow

A world renowned brand, an enterprise power house for the software and hardware of air travel experiences utilizes SharePoint for a part of their info chain. Now a bit more euphemistically - SharePoint is limited unless you have heavy back end ability.
It's not meant to be something as sophisticated as Webflow which is by far more than simple information link hierarchy building.

My job was to set up a pocket of that hierarchy for a key office in the Netherlands.
‍
Quantitative research was king here, stakeholder interviews, and questionnaires helped to identify common cross-team issues, usual work flows, and individual and team preferences.

What are the behavioral patterns and habitual operations of employees?

Familiarity via context

Even though the redesign was entirely digital, exploring the office grounds, laboratories, workshops, etc contributed to the understanding of the types of emotions employees bring into the system.I launched a day in the life type studies to get a full picture.

SharePoint permits either an outdated or minimalist type of IA and navigation.

Minimalism depends on the level of UX content, copywriting and knowing the semantic language. With weak link scent people won't know what's behind nav links leading to major errors, distraction and frustration.

By learning the product technology early on you know the constraints which gives an opportunity to think about future issues, therefore making content and language a priority in user research before even beginning with prototyping.
‍
Used Adobe XD to create some research templates, journey maps and wireframes.
Another way to prevent confusion in navigation or IA is to differentiate information processing - I made relevant icon thumbnails that help engage the right people and habituate the flow.
‍
The wireframes were later used in SharePoint to build the environment.
