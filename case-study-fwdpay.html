Design Vision

Deployment Matters - an incubator tech startup was past having an MVP and needed to build the entire design infrastructure, map out the vision and redesign the existing website into a multi-stakeholder platform.
‍
Technology Catalogue was to offer a holistic catalogue of techs for the global energy sector, and a system built for 3 groups - people who display, install or subscribe to certain technologies.

Path Forward

Working with marketing, business and development meant I had to systematize viewpoints, create reports and optimize deliverables. Working with agile meant daily meetings and collaborative sessions about practices, procedures and changes.
I had to build a fully responsive web styleguide.  I chose appropriate baseline grid, element sizes, typography and interaction states.

The creative lead role offered several challenges: deeply explore platform creation, upkeep and optimization, present the interconnections of the vision in the making, and redesign most of the pages on the website with an accentuation on usability.

The majority of work was hi-fi interface building for the web. Each design stage was performed by myself.

3 sprints were devoted to research, predominantly quantitative to explore high value concepts for product features, design strategies to excel business and user needs , and facilitate a vision for long-term global competitive growth. Leading meant  taking ownership of the entire creative drive of the startup.

Vision Explored

Platform design isn't really too hard in terms of theoretical product planning. The main challenges were to embed mostly meaningful functions in order to facilitate an internal community, then expand that community's ability to communicate with  itself, the business and the other user groups, then universalize behavioral design across the platform. The rest depended more on business planning.
‍
This lead designer role gave me invaluable experience in every design stage. I learned what skills are truly needed for a  senior designer, I expanded my understanding  of business, psychology, and design, motivating me to produce even more custom resources to streamline, design sprints.

It was a challenge to balance between the analytical and creative since all daily activities required a more analytical approach. By the end of my contract, I had created more than 120 Hifi prototypes and a mostly finished long-term growth vision.
