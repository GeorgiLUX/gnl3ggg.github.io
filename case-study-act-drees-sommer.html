Office Design

A service design adventure starting with ACT - a European fintech powerhouse moving office
to a new building.

They needed an environment that while formal in demeanor and style was free, supportive, and motivational for employee procedures.

This project involved a lot of emotional and behavioral considerations. The first priority was to research two main questions - what are the optional physical requirements for operability and how might we increase employee collaboration, co-creation?

Representation of current office environment

Exploring the context/environment

I needed to find out what are the employee flows and interpersonal dynamics, what their day looks like. Unfortunately due to Covid 19+ remote research was conducted but concept validation or testing the interactivity was limited.

Classically,  stakeholder interviews, research briefs, stakeholder inquiries and desk research sufficed for the job.
‍
In-habitation needs familiarity, so before the big covid bans, the new office was visited to explore the physical space and imagine the opportunities, and see the constraints.

For Humans

A primary concern was looking into how a physical human being would best function in office conditions. All based on clinical and academic research.
A universally optimal blueprint for any medium budget.

Design For Motivation

5 strategies  were created with the intend of a closed ecosystem with circular behavior promoting predominantly extrinsic motivations.

This was done with the following design patterns: Gamification, exclusivity,  scarcity, fixed rewards, social proof, delayed gratification.

Psychological mapping of human motivations helped  create a large part of the creative features.
Using the patterns and adjusting the roles for the environment you are likely to produce the planned behavior in this case a more group activity centric workplace with comfortable activities for individuals and augmenting devices for group productivity.
